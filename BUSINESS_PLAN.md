# Business Plan - Content Moderation SaaS for Africa

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the business strategy for a content moderation SaaS platform targeting African markets where governments are making influencers and page administrators legally responsible for comments on their pages. The platform addresses an urgent legal compliance need while maintaining strong unit economics (87%+ margins) using cost-effective AI models.

---

## Target Markets

### Market Selection Criteria
1. **Legal urgency**: Active enforcement of influencer liability laws
2. **Market size**: Social media user base and influencer economy
3. **Payment infrastructure**: Mobile money penetration and reliability
4. **Market readiness**: Awareness of legal risks and willingness to pay

- Same pricing but in international currency (e.g., $25 vs 15,000 XOF)

**Customer Segmentation:**

**Primary Model: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash**
- Input tokens: $0.15 per 1M tokens
- Output tokens: $0.60 per 1M tokens
- Best balance of performance and cost
- 1M token context window
- Supports text, code, images

- Input tokens: $0.20 per 1M tokens
- Output tokens: $0.50 per 1M tokens

- Average input: ~750 tokens (system prompt + comment text + context)

## Break-even Analysis

### Fixed Costs (Monthly)
- Infrastructure (Cloudflare + domains): $25
- Operations, monitoring, backups: $100
- Contingency (support, tools): $50
- **Total minimum fixed**: ~$175/month

### Conservative Scenario (Higher fixed costs)
- Infrastructure + DevOps: $500
- Marketing tools: $200
- Support software: $100
- Contingency: $200
- **Total with overhead**: ~$1,000/month

### Break-even Calculation

**Minimum break-even (at $175/month fixed):**
- With Starter plan ($18.77 net margin): **10 customers**
- With Pro plan ($51.53 net margin): **4 customers**

**Conservative break-even (at $1,000/month fixed):**
- With Starter plan: **54 customers** = $1,350 revenue
- With Pro plan: **20 customers** = $1,160 revenue
- With mixed portfolio (50% Starter, 50% Pro): **29 customers** = $1,204 revenue

**Conclusion: Break-even between 10-54 customers depending on overhead**

## Risk Analysis and Mitigation

### Risk 1: Payment Integration Complexity

**Risk:** African payment providers vary widely in reliability, documentation, and integration difficulty.

**Impact:** High - Can block entire market entry

**Mitigation:**
- Start with best-documented providers (Paystack, Flutterwave)
- Build abstraction layer for multiple providers
- Use aggregators like CinetPay where possible
- Have backup payment options per country
- Allocate 2-3 weeks for payment testing per integration

---

### Risk 2: Currency Fluctuations

**Risk:** Local currencies (especially Naira, CFA franc) can be volatile vs USD

**Impact:** Medium - Can erode margins

**Mitigation:**
- Price in local currency (customers understand)
- Review pricing quarterly and adjust if needed
- Use payment processors that handle FX risk
- Consider USD pricing for high-value customers
- Build 10-15% FX buffer into pricing

---

### Risk 3: Regulatory Changes

**Risk:** Laws could change, become more/less strict, or not be enforced

**Impact:** High - Core value proposition depends on legal risk

**Mitigation:**
- Monitor legal developments in each country
- Diversify across multiple markets
- Position as "general moderation tool" not just legal compliance
- Add features beyond legal compliance (community management, analytics)
- Build relationships with legal experts for early warnings

---

### Risk 4: AI Model Failures

**Risk:** AI models may have accuracy issues with African languages, dialects, cultural context

**Impact:** High - False positives annoy customers, false negatives create legal risk

**Mitigation:**
- Human review option for flagged content
- Continuous model improvement with customer feedback
- Confidence scores (let users set threshold)
- Detailed explanations for flagged content
- Fallback to multiple models (Gemini + Grok)
- Local language training data collection

---

### Risk 5: Competition from Meta/Facebook

**Risk:** Facebook could build native moderation tools

**Impact:** Medium - Would be free, but also generic

**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage and brand building
- Country-specific legal expertise (Meta won't customize per African country)
- Better mobile money integration
- Superior customer support (local, responsive)
- Platform-agnostic (works across Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter)

---

### Risk 6: Low Awareness of Legal Risk

**Risk:** Influencers may not know about liability laws or care

**Impact:** Medium - Makes customer acquisition harder

**Mitigation:**
- Education-first marketing approach
- Partner with legal experts for credibility
- Free tools (legal compliance checklist)
- PR around enforcement cases (when influencers get arrested/fined)
- Testimonials from respected influencers
- ROI calculator: Cost of fine vs cost of protection

---

### Risk 7: Customer Churn

**Risk:** Customers may stop paying after initial scare passes

**Impact:** Medium - Affects LTV and growth

**Mitigation:**
- Annual billing with discount (lock in for 12 months)
- Continuous value delivery (monthly reports, insights)
- Community and education (keep legal awareness high)
- Add features beyond compliance (engagement analytics, sentiment analysis)
- Lapsed customer win-back campaigns
- Strong onboarding to demonstrate value early

---

### Risk 8: Scale Challenges

**Risk:** Growing too fast could strain operations, support, infrastructure

**Impact:** Medium - Could hurt customer experience

**Mitigation:**
- Gradual country-by-country expansion
- Automate everything possible (onboarding, support, billing)
- Build scalable infrastructure from day 1 (Cloudflare Workers)
- Hire part-time support in each market early
- Document processes for repeatability
- Monitoring and alerting for technical issues
