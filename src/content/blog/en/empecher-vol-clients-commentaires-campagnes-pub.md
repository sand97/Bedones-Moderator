## Why African Campaigns Massively Attract Hijacking

In Ivory Coast and Cameroon, sponsored Facebook/Instagram campaigns generate 60-85% of e-commerce sales. But they also attract professional scammers who steal your prospects in real time.

**Scale of the problem**: 58% of African campaigns experience at least one hijacking attempt.

**Catastrophic case - Abidjan Smartphone Launch**:
- Ad budget: 850,000 CFA, 1,240 comments in 48h
- Without protection: 47 fake WhatsApp contacts posted, 180+ people contacted
- Result: 23 successful scams (customers paid scammers), 67 lost sales
- Total loss: Scams (5.75M CFA customers) + Lost sales (3.35M CFA) + Reputation
- Long-term impact: 4 months to restore trust

**With real-time moderation**: 98% of attempts blocked in < 45 seconds.

## During the Campaign: Minute-by-Minute Protocol

### Hours 0-2 (Launch - CRITICAL)

**100% Surveillance**: DO NOT leave the screen.

**Minute 0**: Publish + Pin official comment
**Minutes 1-10**: Like/respond to first 10 positive comments (sets tone)
**Minutes 10-120**:
- Check ALL new comments
- Hide links/numbers in < 60 seconds
- Respond to questions in < 3 minutes

**Golden rule**: First fake contact visible > 5 min = 15-20 potential victims.

**Success case - Douala Fashion Boutique**:
- Campaign 680,000 CFA
- 840 comments in 6h
- 31 scam attempts detected
- All hidden in < 90 seconds
- Result: 0 victims, 147 real sales, 420% ROI

### Hours 3-24 (Active Surveillance)

**Check every 20-30 min**:
- New suspect comments
- Recent accounts with weird activity
- Creative scam variations

**Respond priority**:
1. Purchase questions (< 5 min)
2. Doubts/hesitations (< 10 min)
3. Compliments (< 30 min)

### Hours 24-72 (Maintenance)

**Check every 2-3h**:
Volume drops but vigilance remains necessary.
