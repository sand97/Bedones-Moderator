## Why Fake Giveaways Proliferate in Africa

With 299 million social media users in Africa, scammers have a massive playground. They leverage your audience to promise gifts in exchange for money or personal information.

**Scale of the problem**: According to a 2025 study, 42% of high-audience African pages were targeted by at least one fake giveaway scam attempt.

**Impact**: This destroys your community's trust and can make you lose customers.

**Real case - Abidjan influencer**: A page with 85K followers lost 12,000 followers in 2 weeks after a fake giveaway in comments scammed 47 people for a total of 2.3 million CFA.

## The 5 Most Common Scam Types

### 1. Fake "guaranteed winner" giveaways

"Congratulations! You won 500,000 CFA. To validate, pay 15,000 CFA processing fees to this number..."

**Target**: Known brand pages, lifestyle influencers

### 2. WhatsApp Business phishing

"To order, contact our WhatsApp Business: +XXX..." (fraudulent number impersonating you)

**Impact**: Your customers pay scammers thinking they are buying from you.

### 3. Fake partnerships

"In partnership with [your brand], win an iPhone by clicking here..."

**Consequence**: Your brand is associated with a scam.

### 4. Mobile Money scams

"Send 5,000 CFA on Orange Money to validate your participation in the draw..."

**Volume**: Represents 35% of scams on African pages.

### 5. Page identity theft

Creation of a fake page almost identical to yours to scam your community.

## How to React in Case of Attack (Emergency Protocol)

### Minute 0-5: Secure

1. **Hide** all fraudulent comments immediately
2. **Report** accounts to Facebook/Instagram
3. **Block** the authors

### Minute 5-15: Communicate

4. **Pin** an official warning comment on the post
5. **Post** a story warning about the ongoing scam
6. **Send** a private message to people who replied to scammers

**Message template**:
"Hello, we detected that you replied to a fake giveaway under our post. WARNING, it's a scam. Do not pay anything. Our only official contacts are in bio. Sorry for the inconvenience."

### Minute 15-30: Protect

7. **Adjust** your filters to automatically block this type of comment
8. **Document**: Screenshots to prove your diligence
9. **Warn**: Explanatory post if the scam affected many people

### If people were scammed

- Encourage them to report to the police (cybercrime)
- Publish an official statement distancing yourself
- Offer a goodwill gesture if your security was insufficient
