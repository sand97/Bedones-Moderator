## Define a Clear and Nuanced Policy

### Basic Principle: Zero Tolerance for Hate, Openness to Criticism

**What is PROHIBITED** (immediate deletion):
- Insults targeting ethnic origin, religion, region
- Physical violence threats
- Dehumanizing remarks ("they are animals", etc.)
- Hateful generalizing stereotypes
- Calls for discrimination or exclusion

**What is ALLOWED** (even if negative):
- Reasoned criticism of practices/policies
- Respectfully expressed disagreement
- Questions even clumsy but sincere
- Constructive contradictory debate

**Example - Fine Line**:
‚ùå "The [ethnic X] are all thieves" ‚Üí Deletion (hateful generalization)
‚úÖ "I didn't appreciate this person's attitude" ‚Üí Allowed (individual criticism)

### The Critical Importance of African Context

The same word can be:
- **Offensive** depending on target (ethnicity, region, religion)
- **Neutral** in another context
- **Affectionate** between members of same communities

**Real C√¥te d'Ivoire Example**:
- Word "foreigner": Can be neutral OR very offensive depending on political/social context
- Local nouchi expression: Sometimes vulgar for outsiders, neutral for Abidjan residents

**Absolute Necessity**: Moderators must know local cultural nuances.

## 4-Step Workflow: AI + Human

### Step 1: Automatic Risk Detection (AI)

**Sensitive Keywords by Country**:
- Ethnic terms to monitor (CI, Cameroon, Senegal)
- Polarizing religious references
- Offensive regional expressions

**Automatic Scoring**:
- High risk (score 8-10/10) ‚Üí Immediate quarantine
- Medium risk (5-7/10) ‚Üí Moderator alert
- Low risk (< 5/10) ‚Üí Publication

### Step 2: Quarantine Ambiguous Cases (< 30 sec)

Comment detected as sensitive = temporarily hidden pending human analysis.

**Advantage**: Limits propagation during investigation (10-60 min).

### Step 3: Fast Human Review by Local Moderator (< 2h)

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Does the comment target a specific group?
2. Is there negative generalization?
3. Is the tone aggressive or reasoned?
4. Does context change the meaning?
5. Does author have history of hate speech?

**Practical Case - Douala Restaurant**:
- Comment: "People from [neighborhood X] never pay their bills"
- AI: Score 7/10 (medium risk)
- Human analyzes: Negative generalization based on geographical origin
- Decision: Hide + private response explaining why

### Step 4: Documented Decision with Clear Reason

Each action must be logged:
- Date/time
- Concerned comment (screenshot)
- AI score
- Human decision (publish/hide/delete/block)
- Specific reason

**Usefulness**: Proof of diligence if ARTCI audit or dispute.

## How AI Can Help Without Blind Censorship

### AI = Detection and Sorting, NOT Final Decision

**Recommended Model**:
- AI detects 95% of potentially hateful content
- AI classifies by risk level
- Human decides on ambiguous cases (15-25% of volume)
- AI learns from human decisions

**Advantages**:
- Reactivity: Detection in < 10 seconds
- Consistency: Same criteria 24/7
- Scalability: Handles high volumes
- Safety: Human avoids excessive censorship

**Case Avoided - Tech Influencer Dakar (38K)**:
- AI detects 15-20 sensitive comments/week
- Human validates 12-15 deletions (truly hateful)
- 3-5 false positives put back online
- Result: Clean page + zero over-censorship + zero dispute

## Metrics to Track Monthly

1. **Number of hateful content detected**
2. **Average processing time**
3. **False positive rate** (wrongly hidden content)
4. **False negative rate** (escaped hate)
5. **Number of reports received**

**Healthy Targets**:
- Time < 2h
- False positives < 8%
- False negatives < 3%
- Reports < 5/month
